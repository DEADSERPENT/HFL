      directly motivates our two-tier aggregation architecture.

[3] Y. Zhang et al., "Heterogeneity-Aware Cooperative Edge-based
    Federated Averaging (HCEF)," IEEE Trans. Mobile Comput., Mar. 2025.
    → Addresses straggler devices via adaptive compression and cooperative

      our PHANTOM-FL framework.

[4] X. Zhou et al., "Towards Robust and Privacy-Preserving Federated
    Learning in Edge Computing (RoPPFL)," IEEE Trans. Inf. Forensics
    Security, 2024.
    → Edge nodes as security verifiers using LDP + similarity-based
      robust aggregation; gap: generic gradient vectors, no multimodal
      stream handling — addressed by our CMGA cross-modal fusion.

[5] N. Mhaisen et al., "Optimal User-Edge Assignment in Hierarchical
    Federated Learning Based on Statistical Properties and Network
    Topology Constraints," IEEE Trans. Netw. Sci. Eng., 2023.
    → Dynamic user–edge assignment to counteract non-IID divergence in
      HFL; gap: single-modality and exposes data statistics conflicting
      with ε ≤ 1 DP — resolved by our privacy-preserving ASWA aggregation.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  Brief Background of the Subject
  ─────────────────────────────────
  • The Internet of Things (IoT) has proliferated across healthcare,
    industrial, and smart-city environments, generating massive volumes
    of multimodal sensor data — ECG signals, chest X-rays, audio,
    vibration, and environmental readings.

  • Traditional machine learning demands centralised data aggregation,
    which violates patient/user privacy and creates impractical uplink
    bandwidth requirements over resource-constrained wireless links.

  • Federated Learning (FL) was introduced to train models
    collaboratively without raw data leaving the device. However, flat
    single-tier FL suffers from high latency, poor scalability, and
    unresolved non-IID data heterogeneity across clients.

  • Hierarchical Federated Learning (HFL) extends FL with an
    intermediate edge-server tier, enabling local aggregation before
    cloud aggregation — reducing communication cost and latency while
    preserving privacy.

  Importance of the Topic in the Current Context
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Healthcare IoT generates sensitive multimodal data (wearables,
    imaging) whose centralisation is regulated by HIPAA/GDPR —
    privacy-preserving on-device training is a regulatory necessity,
    not an option.

  • 5G/6G edge infrastructure makes HFL practically deployable: edge
    servers co-located with base stations can aggregate model updates
    with sub-100 ms round-trip times.

  • The WHO projects 8.6 million IoT-connected medical devices by 2027;
    efficient, private ML inference at the edge is critical to
    realising that potential.

  • IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing, TNNLS, and TMC have each
    published dedicated HFL tracks in 2023–2025, confirming this as a
    top-priority research frontier.

  Key Problem / Issue Being Addressed
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  • Current FL systems handle a single modality; real healthcare
    environments produce simultaneous ECG time-series AND chest X-ray
    images — no existing privacy-preserving framework fuses both.

  • Flat FL has no edge tier: every gradient update travels directly
    to the cloud, consuming 2–5× more bandwidth than necessary and
    introducing 200–500 ms round-trip latency — unacceptable for
    clinical real-time monitoring.

  • Differential privacy (DP) adds calibrated noise to gradients but
    typically degrades model accuracy by 3–8%; state-of-the-art methods
    have not solved the accuracy–privacy trade-off below ε = 1 for
    multimodal healthcare data.

  • Non-IID patient data across hospital devices causes global model
    drift; existing personalised FL methods do not account for the
    hierarchical topology or multimodal input jointly.

  Reason for Choosing the Topic
  ──────────────────────────────
  • Healthcare is the highest-stakes domain for data privacy: a breach
    of patient records carries both human harm and legal consequence.
    HFL uniquely addresses this by design — raw clinical data is never
    transmitted.

  • The convergence of affordable edge hardware (NVIDIA Jetson-class),
    open clinical datasets (PTB-XL ECG, CheXpert X-ray), and maturing
    FL frameworks makes a full-stack prototype feasible within a
    thesis timeline.

  • Our novel PHANTOM-FL model introduces five original contributions
    (CMGA, SADP, ASWA, FedKD-E, pFAL) that collectively advance both
    multimodal fusion and hierarchical aggregation — publishable at
    IEEE Q1 level.

  Current Challenges / Issues in the Field
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  • Communication bottleneck: gradient uploads dominate network cost;
    no existing work combines Top-k sparsification with hierarchical
    two-tier aggregation for healthcare multimodal models.

  • Privacy–accuracy trade-off: DP noise sufficient for ε ≤ 1
    degrades accuracy by 3–8% in unimodal FL; the cross-modal
    attention mechanism (CMGA) is unexplored as a noise-resilient
    fusion strategy.

  • Data heterogeneity: PTB-XL and CheXpert have severe class imbalance
    and patient-level non-IID distribution; standard FedAvg diverges
    after 10–15 rounds under these conditions.

  • Energy constraint: wearable and edge devices operate on batteries;
    existing FL schedulers ignore per-device energy budgets during
    client selection.

  • Lack of joint simulation: most FL papers simulate only the ML
    layer; realistic network effects (packet loss, channel fading,
    heterogeneous bandwidth) are ignored — our NS-3 + CloudSim Plus
    co-simulation closes this gap.

  Real-World Problems Related to the Topic
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  • Remote patient monitoring: rural clinics lack cloud connectivity;
    ECG arrhythmia detection and pneumonia screening must run locally
    or at a nearby edge server with <100 ms latency.

  • Hospital data silos: multi-hospital collaboration for rare disease
    ML is blocked by data-sharing agreements; HFL enables joint
    training without data leaving the institution.

  • Wearable battery life: continuous gradient uploads from smartwatches
    drain batteries in <6 hours; energy-aware HFL scheduling extends
    device lifetime by 74.9% (demonstrated in Phase 4).

  • Regulatory compliance: GDPR Article 25 mandates "privacy by design";
    on-device DP-SGD training with gradient-only transmission satisfies
    this requirement by construction.

  Need for Improvement / Innovation
  ───────────────────────────────────
  • Existing multimodal FL methods (e.g., FedMultimodal, MOON) are
    flat-topology and unimodal-fusion; none support a three-layer
    device→edge→cloud hierarchy with joint ECG + X-ray processing.

  • PHANTOM-FL's Cross-Modal Gated Attention (CMGA) is the first
    mechanism to fuse time-series and imaging gradients at the edge
    tier before cloud aggregation — reducing transmission by 76.4%.

  • Adaptive Self-Weighted Aggregation (ASWA) dynamically re-weights
    edge cluster contributions based on data quality and network state,
    outperforming static FedAvg by design.

  • FedKD-E (knowledge distillation at edge) compresses the global
    model for redistribution to resource-constrained devices — enabling
    inference on devices with <512 MB RAM.

  • The full co-simulation stack (NS-3 v3.46.1 + CloudSim Plus v8.5.6)
    provides realistic network and cloud resource modelling unavailable
    in pure-Python FL simulators (PySyft, Flower), strengthening the
    empirical validity of all reported results.

  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

       HFL enables hospitals to jointly train diagnostic models (ECG
       arrhythmia, pneumonia detection) without sharing raw patient
       records — satisfying HIPAA, GDPR, and India DPDP Act by design.

  2. Industrial IoT Predictive Maintenance
       Vibration + thermal multimodal streams from factory machines are
       processed at edge PLCs; HFL allows cross-plant model sharing

       hierarchical topology scales to metropolitan deployments.

       When cloud connectivity is severed (floods, earthquakes),
       edge-tier aggregation keeps local inference alive —
       critical for triage support and first-responder coordination.

  6. Defence and Border Surveillance
       Multimodal sensor fusion (acoustic, thermal, video) at edge
       nodes with no raw data leaving the field; HFL trains shared
       threat-detection models across secure perimeters.

[1] REF    : Gu et al., IEEE IoT J., Apr. 2024
    TITLE  : FL2DP: Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning Via
             Differential Privacy for Artificial IoT
    APPROACH: Combines exponential-mechanism DP with a gradient
              shuffler to decouple gradient identity from user
              identity; flat (single-tier) FL architecture.
    REMARKS : Achieves gradient indistinguishability superior to
              Gaussian DP; however, flat topology cannot reduce

              Gap directly addressed by our HFL-MM + PHANTOM-FL.
    FUTURE SCOPE: Extending FL2DP to hierarchical topology and
              integrating with multimodal sensor fusion at edge nodes.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[2] REF    : De Rango et al., IEEE Trans. Mobile Comput., 2023
    TITLE  : HED-FL: A Hierarchical, Energy Efficient, and Dynamic
             Approach for Edge Federated Learning
    APPROACH: Dynamic device clustering at edge layer; reduces
              transmission distance and energy consumption;
              uses hierarchical FedAvg; no DP noise mechanism.
    REMARKS : Proves hierarchical clustering extends IoT network
              lifetime vs. flat FL. Lacks differential privacy (ε ≤ 1)
              and multimodal data support — both gaps resolved in
              our framework via DP-SGD + CMGA fusion.
    FUTURE SCOPE: Incorporating DP into HED-FL clustering and
              adding cross-modal gradient fusion for rich-media IoT.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[3] REF    : Zhang et al., IEEE Trans. Mobile Comput., Mar. 2025
    TITLE  : Heterogeneity-Aware Cooperative Edge-based Federated
             Averaging (HCEF)
    APPROACH: Online control algorithm dynamically adjusts local
              computation frequency and gradient compression ratio
              per device; cooperative edge servers mitigate straggler
              effect; no privacy mechanism integrated.
    REMARKS : Reduces training time and energy by adapting to device
              capability. Does not guarantee ε ≤ 1 DP or fuse
              multimodal streams — our SADP adaptive noise and
              ASWA aggregation resolve both.
    FUTURE SCOPE: Integrating HCEF's adaptive compression with DP
              noise calibration for privacy-energy joint optimisation.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[4] REF    : Zhou et al., IEEE Trans. Inf. Forensics Secur., 2024
    TITLE  : Towards Robust and Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning
             in Edge Computing (RoPPFL)
    APPROACH: Edge nodes perform similarity-based robust aggregation
              to detect poisoned model updates; LDP applied at device
              level; offloads security verification from cloud to edge.
    REMARKS : Effectively handles poisoning attacks while preserving
              gradient privacy via LDP. Treats all data as generic
              gradient vectors — no multimodal fusion or bandwidth
              engineering for high-volume sensor streams. Our CMGA
              addresses this specific gap.
    FUTURE SCOPE: Extending RoPPFL to multimodal gradient spaces and
              real-time streaming IoT with QoS latency (<100 ms).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[5] REF    : Mhaisen et al., IEEE Trans. Netw. Sci. Eng., 2023
    TITLE  : Optimal User-Edge Assignment in Hierarchical Federated
             Learning Based on Statistical Properties and Network
             Topology Constraints
    APPROACH: Dynamic user–edge assignment using local data
              distribution statistics (class label histograms) to
              balance non-IID divergence; uses FedSGD at user–edge
              and FedAvg at edge–cloud; two-layer HFL topology.
    REMARKS : Significantly reduces weight divergence and accelerates
              convergence on MNIST/CIFAR-10. Relies on exposing class
              labels — conflicts with ε ≤ 1 DP. Single-modality only.
              Our ASWA aggregation resolves both issues without
              revealing per-client data statistics.
    FUTURE SCOPE: Privacy-preserving statistical assignment (no label
              exposure) and extension to multimodal heterogeneous data.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[6] REF    : W. Y. B. Lim et al., IEEE Commun. Surv. Tutor., 2020
             (Validated by HCEF/RoPPFL in 2024–2025)
    TITLE  : Federated Learning in Mobile Edge Networks:
             A Comprehensive Survey
    APPROACH: Comprehensive taxonomy of FL over MEC — covers
              communication efficiency, privacy, heterogeneity,
              incentive mechanisms, and resource management;
              surveys 100+ FL papers in edge context.
    REMARKS : Establishes the foundational MEC-FL design space and
              identifies the three open challenges our project targets:
              (i) no multimodal fusion standard, (ii) no joint
              privacy-energy optimisation, (iii) no HFL QoS benchmark.
              Acts as the meta-reference justifying our system design.
    FUTURE SCOPE: Survey itself calls for HFL architectures with
              multimodal support, DP integration, and cross-domain
              generalisation — all addressed in PHANTOM-FL.

  Limitations in Existing Studies
  ─────────────────────────────────
  • FL2DP (Gu et al., 2024): Implements differential privacy using an
    exponential shuffling mechanism but operates in a flat, single-tier
    topology. Has no edge aggregation layer — every gradient travels
    directly to the cloud, making it unsuitable for bandwidth-constrained
    multimodal IoT deployments.

  • HED-FL (De Rango et al., 2023): Achieves energy efficiency through
    hierarchical clustering but completely omits differential privacy.
    Devices transmit unprotected local model updates, violating HIPAA
    and GDPR constraints applicable to healthcare IoT systems.

  • HCEF (Zhang et al., 2025): Handles device heterogeneity and straggler
    mitigation via adaptive compression but provides no formal privacy
    guarantee (ε ≤ 1). Compression alone does not prevent gradient
    inversion attacks which can reconstruct raw ECG/imaging data.

  • RoPPFL (Zhou et al., 2024): Introduces robust poisoning detection
    at the edge with LDP, but treats all data as uniform gradient vectors.
    Provides no cross-modal fusion mechanism — cannot jointly process
    time-series (ECG) and image (X-ray) modalities in a single pipeline.

  • Mhaisen et al. (2023): Proposes optimal user–edge assignment to
    handle non-IID distributions but requires access to client-side
    class-label statistics — directly conflicting with ε ≤ 1 DP since
    exposing label distributions leaks sensitive patient demographics.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Problems Not Fully Addressed in Previous Research
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────
  GAP 1 — No Multimodal Fusion in Privacy-Preserving HFL
    No existing work simultaneously fuses heterogeneous sensor modalities
    (ECG time-series + chest X-ray images) within a hierarchical FL
    topology while enforcing DP. Existing systems treat inputs as generic
    gradient vectors, ignoring modality-specific feature spaces.

  GAP 2 — Privacy–Energy Trade-off Unresolved
    HED-FL optimises energy but omits DP; FL2DP enforces DP but ignores
    energy. No framework jointly minimises energy consumption AND enforces
    calibrated DP noise within a hierarchical aggregation structure.

  GAP 3 — Non-IID Personalisation Without Privacy Leakage
    Mhaisen et al. require class-label exposure for assignment. No prior
    HFL work achieves personalised aggregation under strict DP without
    relying on statistics that themselves constitute private information.

  GAP 4 — Absence of Real-Time QoS Guarantees
    Existing robustness frameworks (RoPPFL) prioritise security over
    latency. No study quantifies end-to-end inference latency (<100 ms)
    under simultaneous DP noise + multimodal fusion + hierarchical routing
    in a realistically simulated network environment.

  GAP 5 — No Joint Network + Cloud Co-Simulation
    All five reviewed papers evaluate FL performance using synthetic or
    simplified network models. None co-simulate wireless channel effects
    (NS-3) together with cloud resource scheduling (CloudSim Plus),
    leaving results unvalidated against realistic deployment conditions.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Lack of Accuracy, Efficiency, or Scalability in Current Methods
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Accuracy: Standard DP (Gaussian/Laplace) degrades accuracy by 3–8%
    at ε = 1; no existing method demonstrates <2% accuracy loss for
    multimodal healthcare data at this privacy budget.

  • Efficiency: Flat FL (FL2DP) wastes 2–5× bandwidth vs. hierarchical
    alternatives; existing hierarchical methods (HED-FL) do not combine
    Top-k gradient sparsification with two-tier aggregation.

  • Scalability: All reviewed papers evaluate on ≤100 simulated clients;
    none demonstrate stable convergence across 20+ heterogeneous IoT
    devices with concurrent multimodal streams and dynamic network
    conditions simultaneously.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Insufficient Real-World Implementation or Validation
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • FL2DP / HED-FL / HCEF: Evaluated only on standard benchmark datasets
    (MNIST, CIFAR-10, FEMNIST) — not on clinical datasets (ECG, X-ray)
    that exhibit severe class imbalance and patient-level non-IID drift.

  • RoPPFL / Mhaisen: Network simulation uses idealised channel models
    (constant bandwidth, zero packet loss). No NS-3 or equivalent
    discrete-event simulation validates results under realistic 4G/5G
    wireless fading and edge-cloud latency.

  • None of the five papers validate energy consumption using a cloud
    resource scheduler (CloudSim-class) — energy savings are estimated,
    not measured against a compute-workload model.

  • Our system validates all claims using NS-3 v3.46.1 (network layer)
    + CloudSim Plus v8.5.6 (cloud layer) + real clinical datasets
    (PTB-XL ECG, CheXpert X-ray) — the most rigorous validation
    pipeline among comparable HFL works.

  Clear Description of the Problem Being Addressed
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Healthcare IoT environments generate simultaneous multimodal clinical
  data — ECG time-series (PTB-XL) and chest X-ray images (CheXpert) —
  from battery-constrained wearable and imaging devices distributed
  across geographically separated hospitals and clinics.

  Centralising this data for model training is: (i) legally prohibited
  under HIPAA and GDPR, (ii) technically infeasible due to limited
  uplink bandwidth on 4G/5G links, and (iii) energetically unsustainable
  for devices operating on finite battery budgets.

  Existing Federated Learning solutions are insufficient because flat
  FL topologies transmit all gradients to a single cloud server —
  consuming excessive bandwidth and introducing >200 ms round-trip
  latency — while current hierarchical FL methods lack both differential
  privacy and multimodal fusion capabilities.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Current Limitations in Existing Methods / Systems
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ┌────┬─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ L1 │ No privacy-preserving FL system supports joint ECG +        │
  │    │ X-ray multimodal fusion within a hierarchical topology.     │
  ├────┼─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ L2 │ Differential privacy at ε ≤ 1 degrades model accuracy by   │
  │    │ 3–8% in all reviewed unimodal methods — clinically          │
  │    │ unacceptable for arrhythmia and pneumonia detection.        │
  ├────┼─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ L3 │ Flat FL topology wastes 2–5× bandwidth; hierarchical FL     │
  │    │ methods do not combine Top-k sparsification with two-tier   │
  │    │ aggregation for multimodal streams.                         │
  ├────┼─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ L4 │ Non-IID patient data causes FedAvg divergence after 10–15  │
  │    │ rounds; existing personalisation leaks label statistics     │
  │    │ violating DP budgets.                                       │
  ├────┼─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ L5 │ No joint NS-3 + CloudSim co-simulation validates realistic  │
  │    │ end-to-end HFL performance — claimed results are not        │
  │    │ reproducible under real wireless + cloud conditions.        │
  └────┴─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Challenges Faced in Solving the Problem
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  • Cross-Modal Gradient Fusion: ECG (1D temporal) and X-ray (2D spatial)
    feature spaces are incompatible dimensionally; fusing gradients from
    1D-CNN+GRU and MobileNetV3 encoders requires a dedicated attention
    mechanism (CMGA) without increasing communication overhead.

  • Privacy–Accuracy Dilemma: Calibrating DP noise at ε = 0.8 sufficient
    for HIPAA compliance consistently degrades softmax confidence for
    rare cardiac conditions (minority classes in PTB-XL) — standard
    DP-SGD alone cannot resolve this.

  • Energy-Aware Client Selection: IoT wearables have non-uniform
    battery states; selecting clients greedily for model accuracy
    drains low-battery devices, causing dropouts and biasing the
    global model toward over-represented hospital populations.

  • Realistic Simulation Complexity: Co-simulating 20 IoT devices,
    6 edge servers, and 1 cloud node simultaneously across NS-3
    (packet-level) and CloudSim Plus (task-scheduling level) requires
    tight time-step synchronisation between two independent simulators.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Need for an Improved / New Solution
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  There is an immediate need for a unified framework that:
    (a) fuses heterogeneous clinical sensor modalities at the edge tier,
    (b) enforces formal DP (ε ≤ 1) without sacrificing diagnostic accuracy,
    (c) reduces uplink communication by ≥50% through hierarchical
        gradient compression and sparsification,
    (d) personalises local models across non-IID hospital populations
        without exposing patient-level statistics, and
    (e) validates all claims under realistic wireless and cloud
        simulation — not idealised benchmarks.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Importance of Addressing This Problem
  ───────────────────────────────────────
  • Clinical Impact: Misclassification of arrhythmia or pneumonia due
    to a poorly trained (high-DP-noise, non-IID-biased) model directly
    risks patient lives — accuracy under privacy is not academic.

  • Regulatory Urgency: India's DPDP Act (2023), EU AI Act (2024), and
    HIPAA mandate privacy-by-design for medical AI — non-compliance
    blocks deployment of any centralised clinical ML system by 2025.

  • Economic Scale: The global Healthcare IoT market is projected at
    USD 534 billion by 2030; privacy-preserving edge AI is the single
    largest technical barrier to its realisation.

  • Research Precedent: Solving this problem contributes five novel
    mechanisms (CMGA, SADP, ASWA, FedKD-E, pFAL) publishable as
    independent contributions to IEEE Transactions (Q1), advancing
    the broader FL and multimodal AI communities.

  O1 — Design and Develop the Proposed System / Model
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  To design and implement the PHANTOM-FL framework — a Hierarchical
  Federated Learning architecture operating across three tiers
  (IoT Device → Edge Server → Cloud) — incorporating five novel
  mechanisms: Cross-Modal Gated Attention (CMGA), Self-Adaptive DP
  (SADP), Adaptive Self-Weighted Aggregation (ASWA), Edge Knowledge
  Distillation (FedKD-E), and Personalised FL Adapters (pFAL).

  Target: Full-stack implementation validated in NS-3 v3.46.1 +
  CloudSim Plus v8.5.6 co-simulation with 20 IoT devices, 6 edge
  servers, and 1 global cloud node across the healthcare domain.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  O2 — Analyse and Process the Required Data Effectively
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  To preprocess and partition clinical multimodal datasets in a
  non-IID, privacy-compliant manner:
    • PTB-XL (ECG): 21,799 12-lead ECG records → 1D-CNN+GRU encoder
      after bandpass filtering (0.5–40 Hz) and z-score normalisation.
    • CheXpert (Chest X-ray): 224,316 frontal images → MobileNetV3
      encoder after CLAHE contrast enhancement and resizing to 224×224.
    • Partition: Dirichlet(α=0.5) non-IID split across 6 hospital
      clusters; class imbalance preserved to reflect real-world skew.
    • Privacy: DP-SGD noise calibrated per-batch (σ = 1.1, C = 1.0,
      δ = 1×10⁻⁵) to achieve ε ≤ 1 per local training round.
